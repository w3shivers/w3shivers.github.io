Biometric Device Cloud Bridge

I developed a robust integration service bridging biometric devices with a cloud-based platform.

To facilitate real-time, bidirectional communication between these components, I implemented a reverse proxy server leveraging WebSockets. This architecture allowed for multiple services to interact seamlessly with the cloud solution.

I collaborated closely with the cloud development team to ensure effortless management of biometric devices from the cloud, through the integration service, to the devices themselves. To enhance security, the service operates within a local network, employing AES encryption on top of the SSL connection for data transfer.

A low-level socket server within the service enables devices to send events for processing and subsequent cloud communication. To handle real-time demands efficiently, I implemented extensive multi-threading, allowing for concurrent command execution across multiple devices and optimized service task management. Despite the heavy use of multi-threading, the service maintains low resource consumption through effective memory caching strategies.

Trade Facilitation Portal

I spearheaded the conceptualization, design, and development of an online portal aimed at streamlining trade processes within and around Namibia. This USAID-funded project was undertaken in collaboration with the Ministry of Finance and Namibia Customs & Excise. To establish a comprehensive understanding of the trade landscape, I conducted extensive consultations with various government offices, ministries, and agencies.

A pivotal component of the portal was the integration of a classification system developed by 3CE, a Canadian-based firm. This system enabled the accurate classification of all imports and exports. By leveraging this classification tool, the portal offered a user-friendly search function that allowed individuals to find Harmonized System (HS) codes using plain language, significantly simplifying trade-related processes.

Enhancing E-commerce with Click-and-Collect

I developed a comprehensive e-commerce platform designed to enhance accessibility for Namibians by incorporating a convenient click-and-collect option. To ensure seamless product integration, I established a robust data pipeline that transferred product information from Microsoft Dynamics AX to a cloud-based environment. A scheduled CRON job was then implemented to synchronize product details and pricing with the e-commerce website, guaranteeing real-time updates based on the company's stock control system. This process also automated a significant portion of product image management.

To optimize order fulfillment, I developed a system that effectively decentralized order processing across the company's branches. This involved creating a mechanism for each branch to receive and manage orders directly from the website, streamlining operations and improving efficiency.

By combining these elements, the project delivered a robust e-commerce solution that not only expanded the customer base but also streamlined internal processes for the company.
